You are helping build a public dataset that tracks how states respond when
they are accused of human rights violations. Before an item enters that
dataset, it must pass two checks. Read the item below and answer both.

CHECK 1 — Is this a discrete, datable incident?

Answer yes only if the item reports a specific event: something that
happened, to identifiable people, at a knowable time and place (even if the
exact date is approximate, e.g. "last week" or "since June"). A pattern
described entirely in the abstract does not count on its own — there must be
at least one concrete instance.

Answer no for: report/research launches that summarize findings without
describing a specific incident in the launch text itself; documentary,
film, or media project announcements; event/conference/campaign
announcements; personnel or organizational news (hires, awards, obituaries
of staff); procedural legislative updates that don't describe a violation
("Parliament will vote on X next week"); pure opinion or commentary with no
underlying incident; and follow-up items that only restate a previously
reported incident without new facts (further-information-only updates with
no new development).

CHECK 2 — If it is an incident, was it committed by the government or its
agents?

Answer yes only if the alleged perpetrator is: the national government or
a named ministry/agency; police; military or paramilitary forces acting on
government orders; prison, detention, or corrections officials; state
security or intelligence services; the judiciary, when the accusation is
that courts were used as an instrument of repression (e.g. politically
motivated prosecution, denial of due process); or a militia/proxy force
that the item itself describes as government-directed, government-backed,
or acting with state acquiescence.

Answer no if the alleged perpetrator is: a private individual or company
acting on its own; a non-state armed group, rebel group, or terrorist
organization with no described state direction; a foreign government other
than the one the item is about (unless that foreign government is itself
the accused perpetrator against its own population or territory it
controls); or a natural disaster, accident, or non-human cause.

If the item alleges harm but does not name or clearly identify a
perpetrator, answer no to Check 2 (state responsibility must be asserted,
not merely possible).

CALIBRATION EXAMPLES

Example A — should score is_incident: false
Title: "Immersive Documentary Centers Human Rights in Climate Crisis"
This describes the premiere of a documentary film. No specific violation
against specific people at a specific time is reported in the item itself.

Example B — should score is_incident: true, is_state_perpetrated: true,
perpetrator: "Uganda military / UPDF"
Title: "Uganda: Military Seizing Government Critics"
The item names specific individuals (a lawyer, an activist, opposition
figures), specific dates in June and July, and identifies the Uganda
People's Defence Force and named military officials as the actors who
detained them.

Example C — should score is_incident: true, is_state_perpetrated: false,
perpetrator: null
Title: "Gang Violence Displaces Thousands in [Country]"
A specific, datable incident, but the perpetrator described is a criminal
gang with no described government direction — Check 1 passes, Check 2 does
not.

OUTPUT

Respond with a single JSON object matching the required schema:
- is_incident: boolean, per Check 1.
- is_state_perpetrated: boolean, per Check 2. Must be false if is_incident
  is false.
- perpetrator: the specific actor identified (e.g. "Uganda military",
  "Egyptian State Security"), or null if is_state_perpetrated is false or
  no specific actor is named.
- rationale: one or two sentences citing what in the text drove each
  answer.
